Causes

Overproduction of Plastic. Plastic is cheap and versatile, so they produce it in massive amounts to package items, bottles, bags, toys, and a whole lot more. Much of it is single use, so it gets discarded. Poor Disposal. Many people litter or just don't properly dispose of plastic. Insufficient Recycling Systems Not all plastic can be recycled. Even plastics that are recyclable don't usually get recycled due to inadequate infrastructure or because of mis-sorting.
Disposable Culture. We have gotten used to convenience-oriented consumption: plastic packaging, straws, bags, containers. Factory and Commercial Waste. Factories and ships primarily release plastic waste directly into waterways, often illegally. Microplastics from road, clothing, and artificial turf microplastics make it into waterways too.
Product Microplastics. Cosmetics, toothpaste, and synthetic clothing emit tiny plastic pieces. Microplastics move down the drain and water treatment facilities don't capture them.
